The History

Thata-Bosiu cultural vilage is located near the historic Thaba-Bosiu mountain, which is the birthplace of the Basotho nation. In 1824, King Moshoeshoe I settled on the mountain and use it as a stronghold to protect his people during the times of war. The mountain's naturl cliffs made it difficult for enemis to ttack, helping the Basotho urvive and unite as one nation. Today, the cultural village preserves this important history through a museum, traditional huts and guided tours that teach visitors about Basotho culture and heritage.

The site is very significant because it represents unity, leadership and the origin of the Basotho people. It is also a national mountain monument and one of the most important tourist attractions in Lesotho, showing how the nation was formed and how its culture has been preserved over time. Again, Thaba-Bosiu is serving as the 19th century mountain strongheld of King Moshoeshoe. Located about 24 kilometers east of Maseru town, this sandstone platean-whose want means "mountain at night" remaens a powerful symbol of nation.
